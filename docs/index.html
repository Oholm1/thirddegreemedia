I didn’t start with mythos. I started with confusion.

I wanted to learn how to code—simple as that. But every tutorial, every tool, every login came with a shadow: trackers, telemetry, surveillance defaults. So I started asking questions. Then I started building.

This site is the result of that journey. Not a manifesto. Not a movement. Just one person trying to understand the systems we live inside—and how to reclaim a little autonomy from them.

I learned privacy hygiene because I had to. I built infrastructure rituals because the defaults felt wrong. I wrapped it all in absurd lore because it made the work feel alive.

If you’re here to learn, question, or build—welcome. You don’t need to believe in pine-scented VPN tunnels or DNS ley lines. You just need to care about the signal.

This page is the quiet part. The rest of the site is loud on purpose.

We live in a digital landscape where companies like Google, Meta, and Amazon harvest staggering amounts of data—location, behavior, identity—often without meaningful consent. Surveillance capitalism isn’t just invasive; it’s structural. It’s baked into the defaults, hidden behind convenience, and monetized at scale.

Privacy isn’t a luxury—it’s a prerequisite for autonomy. That’s why this site rejects trackers, telemetry, and algorithmic profiling. It’s why I use open-source tools, self-hosted infrastructure, and encrypted channels. Not because it’s easy, but because it’s necessary.

Peer-to-peer sharing is part of that resistance. It’s how we reclaim distribution, decentralize control, and build networks that honor trust instead of extract it. Whether it’s music, knowledge, or infrastructure rituals, P2P is the backbone of digital sovereignty.

This site is a node in that network. A quiet signal in the noise. A place to learn, question, and build without being watched.

🛡️ uBlock Origin — The Glyphic Shield

uBlock Origin isn’t just an ad blocker—it’s a sovereign filter forged in open-source fire. No telemetry. No corporate strings. Just raw signal filtering through recursive lists.

  • Open Source: Auditable, transparent, and community-driven.
  • Zero Tracking: No data collection, no analytics, no whispered secrets.
  • Lightweight: Minimal CPU and memory usage—even with heavy blocklists.
  • Customizable: Fine-tune filters, block scripts, and summon your own rules.
  • Cross-Browser: Firefox, Chromium, Edge, Opera, and more.

Whether you’re browsing absurd rituals or routing traffic through Malmö, uBlock Origin keeps your signal clean and your glyphs uncorrupted.

🧰 NoScript — Script Control for Power Users

NoScript blocks JavaScript and other active content by default, letting you whitelist only the domains you trust. It’s open source and maintained by Giorgio Maone.

  • Granular control: Per-site allow/deny for scripts, objects, media.
  • Hardening extras: Anti-XSS and clickjacking protections.
  • Pairs well: Use alongside uBlock Origin for network-level blocking.

Heads-up: It will break many modern sites until you allow just the minimal domains needed. This is for people who enjoy tuning.

Get it: noscript.net (Firefox/Chromium).

🐧 Why Linux Deserves Your Attention

Linux isn’t just an operating system—it’s a movement. Built on the principles of openness, collaboration, and user freedom, Linux powers everything from smartphones and servers to supercomputers and satellites. Whether you're a developer, a privacy enthusiast, or just someone who wants to break free from corporate ecosystems, Linux offers a customizable, secure, and endlessly fascinating alternative.

🔓 What Makes Linux Special

  • Open Source: You can view, modify, and redistribute the code. No gatekeepers.
  • Community-Driven: Thousands of contributors worldwide keep it evolving.
  • Privacy-Respecting: No telemetry, no tracking—just raw control.
  • Lightweight & Fast: Perfect for older machines or high-performance setups.
  • Secure by Design: Built with multi-user architecture and strong permission systems.

🧠 Popular Linux Distributions

  • Ubuntu – User-friendly and great for beginners.
  • Arch Linux – Minimalist and customizable for power users.
  • Fedora – Cutting-edge features with Red Hat backing.
  • Kali Linux – Tailored for cybersecurity and ethical hacking.
